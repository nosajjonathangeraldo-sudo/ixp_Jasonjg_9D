Thermal Expansion

States of Matter

Matter is made of atoms and molecules and can exist in different forms. The three main states are solid, liquid, and gas.

Solid

Characteristics:
- High density
- Regular pattern
- Vibrate in fixed position
- Low energy level

Liquid

Characteristics:
- Medium density
- Random arrangement
- Move around each other
- Higher energy level than solid

Gas

Characteristics:
- Low density
- Random arrangement
- Move quickly in all directions
- Highest energy level

Brownian Motion

What is Brownian Motion
Brownian motion is the random movement of particles caused by collisions with smaller molecules.

Convergent of Temperature Scales

Why Kelvin Is the SI Unit

Kelvin starts at absolute zero (0 K), the point where molecular motion stops. Because of this, it directly measures thermal energy.

Anomalous Expansion of Water

Water expands when cooled from 4°C to 0°C. This makes ice less dense, so it floats.

Thermal Expansion in Solids and Liquids

Thermal expansion is the increase in size when heated.

Thermal Expansion in Solids

Solids expand when heated because particles vibrate faster and move farther apart.

Gas Pressure

Gas pressure is caused by collisions of gas particles with container walls.
